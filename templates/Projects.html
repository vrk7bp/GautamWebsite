Geography Toy for Elementary Aged Students

Fall 2011 [Introduction to Engineering Class]

My first real, overwhelming introduction to engineering and college came in the form of my first engineering class, ENGR 1620 (Introduction to Engineering). For the final project of the semester, me and a group of four other students were tasked with making a physical, educational toy for elementary aged students. As easy as this sounded, the overall scope of the project became apparent very quickly. Coordinating the programming of the toy with the physical aspect, while also making sure to fulfill the proper standards of learning in Virginia and including feedback from multiple sources, was extremely challenging. Although it there were some of bumps in the road and a lot of sleepless nights, I learned a lot from the project in the end. On the programming end, the entire project was built in JAVA. Our toy was a Geography Toy that asked questions related to continents for students to answer. The students could physically select a continent by moving an arrow over what they believed the answer was (we coordinated with RFID tags in order to realize which continent was which). A GUI with a picture of the World Map, the question, and a students points was shown on a computer screen. As the student answered questions, the questions generated would be adjusted based on the percieved level of the student. Unfortunately, I did not know the magic of GitHub or the importance of neat code at this point in my coding career. The code is really hard to follow and is very honed into the problem that we had.
